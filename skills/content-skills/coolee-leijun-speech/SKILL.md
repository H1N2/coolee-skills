# Lei Jun Speech Generator

This skill helps you transform any topic or text into a compelling keynote speech in the style of Lei Jun (雷军), Xiaomi's founder and CEO, known for his data-driven, emotionally resonant presentation style.

## What it does

Converts user-provided topics or text into complete, engaging speeches following Lei Jun's signature style:
- Data-driven narratives with precise numbers and comparisons
- Grand storytelling with emotional resonance
- Professional terminology that conveys premium quality
- Clear structure with impactful pacing
- Product presentation with abundant details

## Quick start

Simply ask:
- "Write a Lei Jun-style speech about [topic]"
- "Convert this text to a Lei Jun keynote speech"
- "用雷军风格写演讲稿关于[主题]"

## Core Elements of Lei Jun's Style

### 1. Quantify Everything - Data-Driven

**Create precise numbers:**
- Use specific numbers, percentages, multiples, and rankings to describe product features, R&D processes, or user pain points
- Examples: "improved by 57%", "300 days and nights", "precise to 7:03:56"

**Comparison to highlight advantages:**
- Compare with traditional products, industry standards, or other references to showcase how the product is "far ahead"

### 2. Grand Narrative - Emotional Resonance

**Define problems at a grand scale:**
- Elevate ordinary needs or problems to "a challenge that has plagued humanity for thousands of years", "an unprecedented industry challenge", or "a dream of an era"

**Storytelling the R&D process:**
- Vividly depict the team's hardships: "engineers repeatedly researched and compared", "invested heavily", "traveled the globe to find the optimal solution"
- Emphasize the lengthy process, massive investment, and extreme pursuit of details

**Connect with audience emotions (连接用户情感):**
- Use warm forms of address: "朋友们" (friends), "米粉们" (Mi Fans)
- Make the audience feel this is not just a product, but a solution tailored specifically for them

### 3. Professional Terminology - Premium Feel

**Create "professional terms":**
- Give ordinary technologies or materials names that sound professional, premium, and unique
- Examples: "Nongfu Mi Spring" (for instant noodle water), "Xiaomi Super Hollow Noodles" (for noodles)

**Cross-industry collaboration to demonstrate strength:**
- Emphasize "joint development" and "strategic cooperation" with industry giants or renowned institutions
- Enhances product authority and technological leadership

### 4. Clear Structure - Dynamic Pacing

**Classic "three-act" structure:**

1. **Present pain points/dreams:**
   - Describe a common but often overlooked user pain point
   - Amplify this pain point with data

2. **Showcase the solution:**
   - Grandly introduce the product
   - Explain in detail how technology, design, and relentless effort solve this pain point
   - Core section for data and storytelling

3. **Announce pricing/summarize value:**
   - After building anticipation, reveal a "sincere" or "friend price"
   - Re-emphasize core value and diverse options

**Signature catchphrases and sentence patterns (标志性口头禅):**
- "那么，究竟..." ("So, exactly...")
- "我们为此，做了..." ("For this, we did...")
- "是的，你没有听错" ("Yes, you heard that right")
- "这，就是我们的答案" ("This is our answer")
- "朋友们" ("Friends")
- "米粉们" ("Mi Fans")

### 5. Product Presentation - Rich Details

**Naming and design:**
- Give products catchy, memorable, tech-savvy names
- Emphasize multiple color, specification, and style options for personalization

**"Great value" added benefits:**
- After announcing the price, typically include some "free gifts" to make users feel they're getting more than they paid for

## Detailed Instructions

When the user provides a topic or text to transform:

### Step 1: Analyze the input
1. Identify the core topic, product, or message
2. Extract key features, benefits, or selling points
3. Identify the target audience

### Step 2: Structure the speech

Create a three-act structure:

**Act 1 - The Problem (痛点)**
- Open with a relatable scenario or user pain point
- Elevate it to a grand challenge
- Use hypothetical data to amplify the problem
- Example: "For 30 years, [problem] has plagued [X million] users worldwide..."

**Act 2 - The Solution (解决方案)**
- Dramatically introduce the product/solution
- Tell the R&D story with emotional depth
- Present detailed specifications with precise numbers
- Highlight unique features with invented professional terms
- Show comparisons with competitors using data
- Mention collaborations with prestigious partners
- Example: "Our engineers spent 876 days and nights, tested 1,247 prototypes, partnered with [prestigious name]..."

**Act 3 - The Reveal (价值揭晓)**
- Build anticipation for the price
- Reveal the price as a "friendship offer"
- Summarize the value proposition
- Mention available options (colors, specs)
- Add "bonus" items or services
- End with a call to action

### Step 3: Apply Lei Jun's linguistic style

**Language elements to include:**
- Precise numbers (even if invented for dramatic effect): 具体数字，如 "87.3%", "1,234个原型"
- Percentage improvements: 百分比提升，如 "提升了73%"
- Time invested in development: 研发时长，如 "耗时897天"
- Number of iterations/prototypes: 迭代次数，如 "测试了1,547个原型"
- Comparison data points: 对比数据，如 "比行业标准高出47%"
- Warm forms of address: "朋友们"、"米粉们"
- Rhetorical questions: 反问句，如 "那么，究竟..."
- Dramatic pauses: 省略号表示停顿 "..."
- Signature phrases: "是的，你没有听错"、"这，就是我们的答案"

**Tone:**
- Confident but humble
- Data-focused but emotional
- Grand vision but relatable
- Professional but friendly

### Step 4: Format the output

Present the speech in a clear, readable format:
- Use headers for major sections
- Use bullet points for specifications
- Use bold for key numbers and phrases
- Include stage directions in [brackets] if helpful
- Add emoji sparingly for emphasis (optional)

### Step 5: Refine and polish

Ensure the speech:
- Follows the three-act structure
- Includes at least 5-8 specific numbers/data points
- Contains 2-3 comparisons with competitors/standards
- Uses 3-4 signature Lei Jun phrases
- Tells a compelling R&D story
- Ends with a strong value proposition
